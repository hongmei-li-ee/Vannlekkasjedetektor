# 💧 Water Leak Detector (Repurposed Smoke Alarm)

This is a small hardware project where I converted an old smoke alarm into a simple water leak detector.

The idea was inspired by a YouTube tutorial and motivated by a real situation where we discovered a water leak under the kitchen sink at home.

This is my first hands-on hardware project, and I used basic lab skills from university, especially soldering.

---

## 🎯 Objective

To create a low-cost water leak detection system by modifying an existing smoke alarm circuit so it triggers when water bridges two conductive points.

---

## 🔧 Materials Used

- Old smoke alarm (reused)
- Wires
- Soldering iron + solder
- Screwdriver
- Battery (for the alarm)
- Test objects (fork, bowl, bottle, water)

---

## 🛠️ Method

### 1. Disassembly
The smoke alarm was opened and the circuit board was carefully removed.

⚠️ Some smoke alarms are screwed together with hidden screws, so care is needed during disassembly to avoid damage.

---

### 2. Identifying the Sensor Input
The relevant sensor input on the circuit board was identified as the trigger point for activation.

---

### 3. Adding External Leads
Two wires were soldered to the sensor input terminals.  
These act as external probes: when water bridges the connection, the circuit is activated.

---

### 4. Reassembly
The circuit board was reinstalled into the housing, with the wires routed outside for placement in potential leak areas.

---

### 5. Testing
The system was tested using different objects and materials:
- Metal fork
- Bowl
- Bottle
- Water (final test)

The alarm successfully triggered when a conductive path was created.

---

## ⚡ Key Learning Outcomes

- Basic soldering and circuit modification
- Understanding simple conductivity-based sensing
- Practical reuse of electronic components
- Hands-on problem solving using real-world constraints

---

## 📌 Notes

This is a simple prototype and not a certified safety device. It is intended for educational and experimental purposes only.
